Samulnori

사물놀이

Instruments Used

Kkwaenggwari 꽹과리

A small hand-held brass gong that leads the ensemble with bright, sharp rhythmic signals.

Jing 징

A large gong with a deep, sustained sound that supports the ensemble and marks important beats.

Janggu 장구

An hourglass-shaped drum played on both sides to create contrasting tones and detailed rhythms.

Buk 북

A barrel drum that provides a strong, grounded pulse and adds weight to the ensemble.

Instruments Used

Kkwaenggwari 꽹과리

The lead gong guides tempo changes, cues formations, and drives the energetic character of Nongak.

Jing 징

The large gong adds broad, resonant tones that balance the sharper sounds of the ensemble.

Janggu 장구

The double-headed drum performs flowing rhythmic patterns while supporting movement and dance.

Buk 북

The barrel drum reinforces the central beat with powerful, steady strokes.

Sogo 소고

A small hand drum used while dancing, combining percussion with expressive movement.
